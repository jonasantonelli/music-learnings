## Study Notes

**Bars 1–4 (Cm7):** The song sits on Cm7 for four bars. Use C Dorian or C minor pentatonic. Coltrane favors a bluesy, sustained approach — long tones and space work well here.

**Bars 5–6 (Fm7):** IV chord. Shift to F Dorian or F minor pentatonic. Think of it as a minor subdominant move — the blues IV.

**Bars 7–8 (Cm7):** Return to the tonic. Re-establish the C minor feel before the turnaround.

**Bars 9–10 (Ab7 → G7b9):** Chromatic tritone-to-dominant turnaround. Ab7 is the bVI7 — a flat-side dominant that voice-leads down a half step into G7b9. Use Mixolydian or the Lydian dominant (Ab Lydian b7) on Ab7. On G7b9, the diminished scale (half-whole) or harmonic minor V mode are both idiomatic Coltrane choices.

**Bar 12 (G7b9):** Turnaround — sets up the return to Cm7. Build tension with the b9 and b13 of G7 before resolving back to the top.
