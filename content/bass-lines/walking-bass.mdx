Walking bass is a quarter-note-driven technique that outlines chord changes one
note at a time. Originally a double bass staple, it's essential vocabulary for
jazz guitarists playing in duo or solo settings.

### The rules

- **Beat 1** — land on the root to anchor the harmony
- **Beats 2 & 3** — fill with chord tones (3rd, 5th, 7th) to outline the quality
- **Beat 4** — chromatic approach note (half step above or below) targeting the next chord's root

### Practice tips

Start slow with a metronome at 60 BPM and loop a single ii-V-I until the
patterns sit under your fingers. Once comfortable, try connecting patterns —
use pattern 1 on the ii, pattern 3 on the V, and so on. The goal is smooth
voice leading between chords, not speed.
