**Mixolydian ♭9 ♭13** — better known as **Phrygian Dominant** — is the 5th
mode of [harmonic minor](/lessons/scales/harmonic-minor). It's a dominant
scale (major 3rd, ♭7) with a ♭9 and ♭13, which makes it the natural choice
over a **V7 chord resolving to a minor i**. Outside of jazz, it's the
fundamental scale of flamenco, much klezmer music, and a lot of Middle
Eastern modal playing.

### Interval formula

| Degree | 1 | ♭9 | 3 | 4 | 5 | ♭13 | ♭7 |
|--------|---|----|---|---|---|-----|----|
| Semitones from root | 0 | 1 | 4 | 5 | 7 | 8 | 10 |

Compared to Mixolydian (1 2 3 4 5 6 ♭7 — the "neutral" dominant scale), the
9th is flatted and the 13th is flatted. The result has a **3-semitone
augmented second** between the ♭9 and the 3rd, which is the signature
flamenco sound.

### Relation to harmonic minor

Phrygian Dominant is the **5th mode of harmonic minor**. E Phrygian Dominant
contains the same notes as **A harmonic minor** — just starting from E
instead of A:

> A harmonic minor: A B C D E F G♯
> E Phrygian Dominant: E F G♯ A B C D

This is the key relationship: **to play Phrygian Dominant over a V7 chord
resolving to minor, use the harmonic minor scale of the destination minor
chord**. Over E7 → Am, play A harmonic minor starting from E.

### Chord applications

- **V7 in a minor key** — the main jazz use. Any time you see a V7 chord
  resolving to a minor i (E7 → Am, G7 → Cm, etc.), Phrygian Dominant is
  the default scale choice over the V7 bar
- **Altered-adjacent dominants** — if you want a ♭9 and ♭13 color but don't
  want the full altered scale (which also alters the 5 and uses a ♯9),
  Phrygian Dominant is the "half-altered" option that keeps the perfect 5
- **Flamenco and modal vamps** — in flamenco music, the "Phrygian cadence"
  is a vamp between a minor iv chord and a major I chord (e.g. Am → E,
  Am → E, ...). The E major chord is treated as the tonic, and Phrygian
  Dominant is the melody scale
- **Middle Eastern and klezmer melodies** — the augmented 2nd is the
  defining sound of the *Ahava Rabbah* mode in Jewish liturgical music and
  *Hijaz* in Arabic maqam

### Practice tips

- **Practice it against a V7 → i minor cadence** — loop E7 → Am and play
  A harmonic minor (= E Phrygian Dominant) over the E7 bar, then resolve
  into A natural or harmonic minor on the Am
- **Internalize the ♭9 to 3 leap** — this is the augmented 2nd, and it's
  the single interval that defines the sound. Sing it. Play lines that
  feature it rather than step around it
- **Try flamenco vamps** — sit on an E major chord and improvise with
  E Phrygian Dominant as if E were the tonic. This is how the scale feels
  outside of jazz V7 context
- **Compare to the altered scale** — both work over a V7 to minor, but
  Phrygian Dominant keeps the natural 5 while altered flats it. Listen to
  the difference and choose based on how much tension you want
