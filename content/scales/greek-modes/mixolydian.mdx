**Mixolydian** is the 5th mode of the [major scale](/lessons/scales/greek-modes/ionian).
It's a major scale with a **♭7**, which makes it the default scale for dominant
7th chords. It's the sound of blues, funk, rock, and every V7 chord in
traditional harmony. If you've ever played over a 12-bar blues, you've used
Mixolydian.

### Interval formula

| Degree | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | ♭7 |
|--------|---|---|---|---|---|---|----|
| Semitones from root | 0 | 2 | 4 | 5 | 7 | 9 | 10 |

Compared to Ionian, only the 7th is lowered. That ♭7 is what makes the
dominant 7th chord.

### Chord applications

- **Dominant 7th chords (V7)** — Mixolydian is the default scale for any V7
  chord resolving to I in a major key
- **Blues** — the Mixolydian ♭7 combined with the major 3rd gives the
  characteristic blues/dominant sound
- **Funk and rock riffs** — static dominant vamps live in Mixolydian territory
- **Avoid note: the 4th** — over a dominant 7th chord, the natural 4 is a half
  step above the 3rd and is typically treated as a passing tone

### Practice tips

- **Hear the ♭7 against the 3** — that tritone between the 3rd and ♭7 is the
  dominant 7th sound; it wants to resolve
- **Play over a blues** — Mixolydian is the "inside" sound over each chord in a
  12-bar blues
- **Compare with Ionian** — same scale, different 7th; Ionian resolves, Mixolydian
  wants to move
- **Compare with [Lydian ♭7](/lessons/scales/jazz-scales/lydian-b7)** — Mixolydian has a
  natural 4; Lydian ♭7 has a ♯4. Mixolydian resolves, Lydian ♭7 floats
